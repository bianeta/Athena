A good girls guide to murder by Holly Jackson

Published: 2019
Genre: Young Adult, Mystery, Thriller
Rating: 4/5
Summary
This book follows 17-year-old Pippa Fitz-Amobi(Pip). Pip has a Capstone Project or EPQ ( depending on the US or UK version you read). Pip has decided that Sal Singh is innocent and didn't kill his girlfriend, Andie Bell ( By the way, Sal is also dead). ( Spoiler alert, she's right! *gasp*) During the process of trying to solve the case. She befriends Sals' brother Ravi. In the end, we find out Elliot Ward( who is Pip's friend's dad, and also teacher). Accidentally gave Andie a concussion. And then Andie's sister, Becca Bell, just watches her die. Becca then shoves her into a tank. Pip finds out this information and gets drugged by Becca Bell. She Survives, and Elliot goes to Jail
Review
I genuinely enjoyed this book. I will say it could be slow at times, though. Maybe I need to reread it, but this book to me a while to get through. Either way, it was amazing. I also cried super hard at Barney's death.

    This book was super good. It is crazy how we got this sort of ending from a case that started as a missing persons case. I really wasn't expecting Stanley Forbes to get dragged into all of this. Without that dang tracker on Pip's phone, she would be dead( Veronica Sterling should take notes and ACTIVATE tracking devices.) I also love how Ravi again makes sure Pip does not push her limit.
